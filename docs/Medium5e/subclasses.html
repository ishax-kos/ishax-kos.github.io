Spiritual Disciplines

Mediation is an ancient practice and it has arisen numerous times in a myriad of forms. Choose a spiritual discipline from those below.

  • Shaman
  • Sha'ir

Your choice grants you features at 1st level and again at 6th, 10th, and 14th level.

Shaman

The shaman's abilities relate to aiding others as much as well as one's self. The shaman's filler spells should come from the druid.

Bonus Proficiencies

When you are first realized as a Shaman at level 1, you gain an additional proficiency with one of the following skills: Animal Handling, Arcana, Medicine, Nature or Survival.

In addition you add all celestials and fey to your repertoire.

Eyes of Mercy

As a free action you can discern if a creature you can see within 30 feet of you is blinded, deafened, fatigued, frightened, paralyzed, poisoned, stunned, unconscious, exhausted or has contracted a disease. If you make physical contact with a creature, you immediately know if it is charmed, petrified or possessed.

Rite of Guidance

Starting at 6th level, you learn to beset helpful or harmful spirits upon those around you. When another creature you can see makes an attack roll, an ability check, or a saving throw, you can use your reaction to affect the result. You may spend a spell slot and apply the spell slot's level as a bonus or penalty (your choice) to the creature's roll. If the bonus or penalty is larger than your proficiency bonus, use your proficiency bonus instead. You can use this ability after the creature rolls but before any effects of the roll occur.

Animus

At 10th level, you can exa make a Charisma (Persuasion or Intimidation) against a dc 10 + the item's enhancement bonus if it has one. If this succeeds, you learn the name of the one who made the item and the name of the place it was made in (from it's maker's perspective). If it is a magic item or some other magic-imbued object, you learn its properties and how to use them, whether it requires attunement to use, and how many charges it has, if any. You learn whether any spells are affecting the item and what they are. If the item was created by a spell, you learn which spell created it.

Revealer

At 10th level, you can designate any divination you cast, to be cast by another within 30 feet.

Sha'ir

A sha'ir is able to contact a myriad of elemental beings at his whim (and theirs) through his djinn (a mephit).

Bonus Proficiencies

When you begin down the path of the Sha'ir at level 1, you gain proficiency in the Performance skill as well as proficiency in one skill from Arcana, Nature and Religion.

In addition you add all elementals to your repertoire.

Shiqq Mediator

You gain the services of a shiqq. In exchange, you partake in a ritual to some day make the shiqq whole. You do this by imbuing it with the elements around you, and a portion of yourself. This forever binds the shiqq to you, and allows it to take shape as you grow stronger.

A shiqq is a small elemental related to the djinn. As the ward of a sha'ir it cannot attack and has the following game statistics:

HP: 3 (1d6), AC: 13, Str: 5, Dex: 16, Con: 10, Int: 7, Wis: 10, Cha: 7, CR 0, Spell Retrieval

Spell Retrieval

In exchange for the opportunity to become whole, the shiqq acts as a go-between to the elemental planes, returning with any spell its master desires. It can be sent off amongst the planes to find a spell for its sha'ir. The shiqq will take an amount of time according to the table Spell Retrieval depending on what spell list the spell comes from. You may cast the spell the same round it arrives, but you cannot hold it longer than 30 minutes. These spells do not cost spell slots to be cast, however you may only ever hold one at any given time.

As an action, you can temporarily dismiss the shiqq. It disappears into a pocket dimension where it awaits your summons. As an action while it is temporarily dismissed, you can cause it to reappear in any unoccupied space within 30 feet of you.

"Huh? What are 'Shiqqun'?"

Shiqqun are lesser djinn that have only half formed. As a result of this tragedy, they are often hideous and small; they are pitied by djinn across the elemental planes. A shiqq is not skilled in magic or combat itself, and will therefore rarely fully enter the material plane to interact with others besides it's sha'ir.

The Spell Retrieval table shows how long it takes the shiqq to return with a spell.

Mephit Status

At 6th level, the shiqqan’s form is finally made whole. Your mediator takes the form of a mephit companion. The type of mephit it becomes is often dependant on where the original ritual was cast, where you have traveled since, or even your own nature; but this is not guaranteed. Your shiqq companion crawls its way into the material plane as it transforms and gains all the benefits of your Companion’s Bond and bonus cantrips abilities. You can have only one mephit companion at a time.

Your shiqq or mephit companion is sure to tell you that if it ever dies, with 8 hours of work, the expenditure of 50 gp worth of rare herbs and incense, and half of your maximum hit points, it can be resurrected. If you cannot recover it's remains, it will not retain it's memories.

Companion's Bond

This mephit gains a variety of benefits while it is linked to you.

The mephit obeys your commands as best it can. It rolls for initiative like any other creature, but you determine its actions, decisions, attitudes, and so on. If you are incapacitated or absent, the mephit acts on its own.

The mephit has abilities and game statistics determined in part by your level. The mephit uses your proficiency bonus rather than its own. In addition to the areas where it normally uses its proficiency bonus, a mephit companion also adds this proficiency bonus to its AC and to its damage rolls. As you gain levels, the mephit companion also gains additional hit dice and increases its hit points accordingly. The mephit companion has a number of hit die equal to your level.

Whenever you gain the Ability Score Improvement class feature, the mephit’s abilities also improve. The mephit can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or it can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, the mephit can’t increase an ability score above 20 using this feature unless its description specifies otherwise.

The mephit gains proficiency in one skill of your choice. It also becomes proficient with all saving throws.

The mephit shares your alignment, though it’s personality traits and impulses are influenced by the behaviors mentioned in it’s monster entry. The mephit is prone to take on your ideal, and is willing to suffer on your behalf within reason.

The mephit retains the Spell Retrieval ability and can now retrieve spells for you once per short rest. You can request one spell of any level for which you have spell slots, though you need not have any remaining.

Elemental Protection

When you reach 14th level, you and the mephit both become more resistant to elemental forces. Each of you gain resistance to two types of damage. These can be acid, cold, fire, lightning or thunder damage. Your choice for the mephit need not match what you choose for yourself.
